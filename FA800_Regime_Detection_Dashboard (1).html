Z-Score Analysis — Rolling Standardization

Phase I

Before regime modeling, LSI and IRI are transformed to rolling z-scores to ensure comparability and remove long-run level drift.

Rolling Z-Score Formula
LSI_Z(t) = [ LSI(t) − μ(t−52, t) ] / σ(t−52, t)
IRI_Z(t) = [ IRI(t) − μ(t−52, t) ] / σ(t−52, t)
52-week (1-year) rolling window. Re-computed each week — the model sees only past data.

Optimization Framework

Phase II

Each regime's portfolio is constructed by maximizing the Sharpe ratio with Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage on regime-conditional covariance estimates.

Objective function
w* = argmax [ wᵀμ / √(wᵀΣw) ]
μ and Σ estimated from regime-specific observations only — the optimizer learns how assets actually behave in each environment.
Covariance estimation
Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage — applied to stabilize the covariance matrix when regime-specific sample sizes are small. Prevents overfitting and unstable weights.
Constraints and economic rationale
wᵢ ≥ 0
Long-only
No short-selling. Reflects institutional mandates.
Σwᵢ = 1
Full investment
All capital deployed. No cash drag.
wᵢ ≤ 60%
Concentration cap
Prevents single-asset dominance.
n ≥ 20
Min. observations
Fall back to equal weights if too few regime weeks.

Key Takeaways

Conclusion
  • 01
    Regimes are economically meaningful Asset return distributions shift materially across Deep Calm, Calm, Elevated Stress, and Crisis — the four states Phase I identified. SPY vol quintuples from deep calm (9.7%) to crisis (46.9%).
  • 02
    Static allocation misses regime dynamics 60/40 ignores how correlations and volatilities change. It works on average (9.88% return) but suffers steep drawdowns (−26.92%) and is inefficient regime-by-regime.
  • 03
    Optimization beats intuition Hand-picked weights (Sharpe 0.877) underperformed both the benchmark and optimized strategies. Systematic optimization via Sharpe-maximization is essential.
  • 04
    Probability-weighting is the strongest approach Blending allocations with HMM state probabilities produced the best Sharpe (1.0237), Calmar (0.471), and lowest volatility (9.39%). The forward-looking signal adds real value.
  • 05
    Gold is the universal crisis hedge GLD is the only asset with positive returns in every regime. In crisis it delivers +24.1% annualized at a Sharpe of 0.88 — justifying its 45% (hand-picked) or 36% (optimized) crisis weight.
  • 06
    Academic positioning: forward-looking advantage Volatility-based regimes are backward-looking. LSI and IRI capture liquidity functioning and forward risk perception jointly — this systemic stress transmission narrative is the core intellectual contribution.
